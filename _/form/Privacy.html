PRIVACY POLICY
PERSONAL DATA PROTECTION ACT 2010

  1. Purpose of this Policy

  1. In compliance with the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (“PDPA 2010”), this PDPA Privacy Policy (“PDPA Privacy Policy”) sets out how Coca-Cola Bottlers Malaysia Sdn Bhd and Coca-Cola Refreshments Malaysia Sdn Bhd (collectively known as “the Company”) will take with respect to the processing of personal data belonging to data subjects and to provide appropriate and consistent safeguards for the handling of such personal data for the purposes of the PDPA 2010.

  2. This PDPA Privacy Policy should be read in conjunction with The Coca-Cola Company Privacy Policy (“TCCC Policy”) and that the Company is bound by both TCCC Policy and PDPA Privacy Policy. Where applicable local law, namely PDPA 2010, provides a lower level of protection of personal than that established by TCCC Policy, then the requirements of the TCCC Policy shall apply.

    When used in this PDPA Privacy Policy:

    “data subject”means any individual including current or former employee, independent worker, temporary agent of the Company, or any employee of an independent contractor, without regard to whether such worker is full-time or part-time, consumer, service provider, distributor, customer or supplier.
    “processing” refers to any action that is performed on personal data, such as collecting, recording, organizing, storing, using, disclosing, transmitting, disseminating, blocking, or deleting such data.
    “Personal Data” means any information in respect of commercial transactions collected and used by the Company that relates directly or indirectly to a data subject including but not limited to name, contact information such as address and telephone number, NRIC number, date of birth, and includes Sensitive Personal Data. It does not include data taken from Government or State bodies OR data processed by a credit reporting agency under the Credit Reporting Agencies Act 2010 OR used outside Malaysia and not in any manner in Malaysia.”
    “Sensitive Personal Data” means Personal Data consisting of information as to the physical or mental health or condition of a data subject, his political opinions, religious or other similar.”

  1. General Principle - Consent

    2. When appropriate or as required by law, the Company will provide data subjects with the form for the right of consent in writing (“Consent Forms”) to choose how, when and for what purpose Personal Data will be collected, used, and disclosed. This will vary depending on the types of data to be collected and the proposed purposes of use.

  2. Notice and Choice - Purpose of Data Collection

    3. The Company will only use Personal Data for the purposes of legitimate business needs, or as otherwise or authorized by law and depending on the classification or category of which individual the data subjects fall into. The detailed description of the purposes of processing Personal Data will be elaborated in the relevant Consent Form

  3. Disclosure

    4. The Personal Data shall not be disclosed for any other purpose other than the purpose for which it was disclosed. The Company may have to disclose and share Personal Data to classes of third parties (eg. external consultants, business partners, appointed distributors, agents, suppliers, vendors and contractors; governmental bodies or authorities).

  4. Security

    5. The Company is committed to taking appropriate and commercially reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect personal data against unauthorized access, accidental loss or damage, and unauthorized destruction.

  5. Retention Policy

    6. The Company will retain Personal Data for a reasonable period in accordance with legal requirements and statutory obligations. The Company shall take reasonable steps to ensure that all Personal Data is destroyed or permanently deleted if it is no longer required for the purpose for which it was to be processed or where a data subject demonstrates or informs the Company that the purpose for which the Personal Data being retained is no longer legal or appropriate.

  6. Data Integrity

    7. The Company commits to take all reasonable steps to ensure that the Personal Data is accurate, complete, not misleading and kept up-to-date, having regard to the purpose for which the personal data was collected and processed.

  1. Access and Accuracy of Personal Data

  1. In order to ensure that Personal Data collected are kept up-to-date and accurate, data subjects have the right to request a copy of their personal information that the Company or the Company’s entity hold about him or her and the right to correct or amend or update any inaccurate or incomplete or changes to their Personal Data the Company holds about him or her
  2. The Company will reply to a data subject’s request and/or correct any inaccuracies of Personal Data no later than twenty one (21) days upon receipt of such aforesaid request except where compelling business interests require otherwise. Requests for access, correction, or deletion may be denied if the law requires, or if the request in unreasonable, or for compelling grounds relating to the particular circumstances. In case the request is denied, the Company shall inform the data subject about its reasons for the denial.

  1. Transfers of Personal Data

    2. The Company strives to ensure a consistent and appropriate level of protection for personal data that are processed and/or transferred between the Company’s entities. All transfers between the Company’s entities will be carried out in accordance with the TCCC Policy and PDPA Privacy Policy. In the event that certain laws impose cross border transfer requirements that are stricter than those described in the PDPA Policy, the Company will handle such transfers in accordance with applicable local law.

  2. Implementation and Modification To PDPA Privacy Policy

    3. This PDPA Privacy Policy will become effective as of 1 August 2013. The Company reserves the right to modify the PDPA Privacy Policy as and when it deems necessary so as to comply with applicable laws.

  3. Complaints and Feedback

    4. The Company and all its entities and employees will ensure compliance of this PDPA Privacy Policy. Noncompliance of the PDPA Privacy Policy may result in disciplinary sanctions, dismissal or any other type of sanction permitted by applicable law.
